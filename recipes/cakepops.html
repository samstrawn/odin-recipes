Cakepops

Fun and Festive Treat for the Holidays!

Ingredients

  • 1 box red velvet cake mix (or any desired flavor but red velvet looks festive)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup frosting
  • 12 ounces candy melts
  • lollipop sticks (optional but fun)
  • sprinkles or other desired decorative toppings

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F. Butter a 13x9 in cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together cake mix, water, oil, and eggs until combined. Pour into pan.
  3. Bake according to package directions. Cool completely.
  4. Line a small baking sheet or plate with wax or parchment paper.
  5. Crumble cooled cake into a large bowl and add frosting. Stir with wooden spoon until texture resembles wet sand.
  6. Scoop 1 tablespoon of cake mixture and roll into balls.
  7. Place on lined plate and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or freeze for 30 mins.
  8. Melt candy according to package instructions and place in a 2 cup measuring cup or tall drinking glass.
  9. Pull out a few cake balls at a time to work on while the rest stay in the fridge. (keeping them cold helps the coating to set quickly.
  10. Dip the tip of a lollipop stick in the melted chocolate and insert about halfway into a cake ball
  11. Dip cake ball into the chocolate, making sure to coat entirely. Remove and let excess chocolate drip off.
  12. Decorate with sprinkles or toppings (optional) and place upright in a styrofoam block or cardboard box. Let stand until coating is set.

    13. *Cakepops can last in the fridge up to 1 week!
